Klein Bottle

The Klein bottle is a four-dimensional surface that only has one side. In this way it's a bit like the three-dimensional Möbius strip but instead of a flat cross-section and one edge the Klein bottle has a circular cross-section and no edges.

If your browser supports WebGL you should see a spinning Klein bottle above. It looks like it's morphing and passing through itself while it spins but that's actually just an artifact of displaying four dimensions on two. Morphing, at least, also occurs when you render three dimensions onto two: a cube's square faces only look square when you look at them straight on; at other angles they're distorted.

After being rotated, the model is Orthographically projected onto two dimensions and shaded using a straightforward generalization of the Phong reflection model.

The details are in the source code, which is free for any purpose (WTFPL). Let me know if you find it useful!
